Optimize Motor Motion Profiling with S-Curve and resolve Sensor Noise

Description of Development:

1. `Motor_Torque_Test.ino` & `Ventilator_Firmware`:
- Identified that the significant DC shift in the MPX5010DP readings was caused by a ground-loop between the motor driver and sensor. Resolved by switching the sensor's GND wire to a dedicated ground pin on the Arduino Uno (star-ground topology), reducing noise by ~20x and eliminating the motor-enable shift.
- Replaced the linear trapezoidal ramp in `_calcStepInterval` with a cosine-based S-Curve profile. This limits jerk by easing acceleration and deceleration, providing smoother mechanical motion and eliminating mechanical jolt to the sensors/Ambu bag.
- Widened the acceleration/deceleration zones to 30% each and lowered the starting speed to make the S-Curve pronounced and visually/audibly obvious.
- Reverted motor and timing constants back to the calibrated 800 PPR settings, confirming that higher microstepping exceeded the ADC conversion overhead.

2. New Diagnostic Tools:
- Added `Flow_Noise_Diagnostic.ino` to isolate EMI/vibration and test motor stepping without bag contact.
- Added `SCurve_Demo.ino` to visually and audibly verify the smooth S-curve motion profile versus the harsh trapezoidal profile.
